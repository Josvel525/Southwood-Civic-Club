Property Maintenance Expectations (General)

Keeping Southwood well-maintained for everyone

Maintaining your property in good condition protects your investment, supports neighborhood property values, and keeps Southwood a welcoming community. The following expectations apply to all lots within Blocks 1–11 of the Southwood Addition.

Lot Maintenance & Landscaping

All lots must be kept clear of excessive weeds, overgrown vegetation, and accumulated debris. Grass and landscaping should be regularly mowed, trimmed, and maintained to a neat appearance consistent with the surrounding neighborhood. Vacant lots are subject to the same maintenance standards as occupied properties.

  • Mow and edge lawns regularly to prevent overgrowth.
  • Remove dead trees, stumps, or limbs that pose a hazard or eyesore.
  • Keep shrubs and hedges trimmed so they do not encroach on sidewalks, streets, or adjacent lots.
  • Dispose of yard waste promptly according to City of Houston bulk trash pickup schedules.

Structural Upkeep

Homes and accessory structures must be kept in a state of good repair. Deteriorating paint, rotting wood, broken windows, damaged roofing, and crumbling masonry are not consistent with community standards and must be addressed promptly.

  • Repaint or refinish exterior surfaces showing significant peeling, fading, or weathering.
  • Repair or replace broken gutters, downspouts, fascia boards, and soffits.
  • Keep porches, steps, railings, and driveways in a safe and structurally sound condition.
  • After fire, storm, or other damage, repairs must be completed within 120 days. Residents experiencing genuine hardship may petition the Housing & Community Development Committee for a time extension.

Driveways & Impervious Surfaces

Driveways must be constructed of concrete meeting City of Houston specifications (minimum 4″ thick). Walks from the street curb to the residence must be at least 4 feet wide. Cracked or sunken driveway sections should be repaired to maintain a smooth, safe surface.

Accessory Structures & Storage

Sheds, garages, carports, and other accessory structures must be maintained in the same manner as the primary residence. They should be structurally sound, free of graffiti, and not used to store materials that create unsanitary conditions or attract pests.

  • Garbage and recycling containers should be stored out of view from the street except on collection days.
  • Outdoor furniture and equipment left in front yards should be in good, usable condition.
  • Grills and barbecue equipment must be stored in the back yard when not in active use.

Livestock & Animals

No livestock, poultry, or farm animals are permitted on any lot within the Southwood Addition. Domestic pets must be kept in compliance with all City of Houston ordinances, including leash laws and vaccination requirements.

Temporary Structures

Trailers, mobile homes, modular units, and other temporary structures may not be placed on any lot and used as a place of permanent habitation. Temporary construction-related structures may be permitted during active permitted construction, subject to applicable city codes.

Questions or Concerns?

If you have questions about maintenance standards or need to report a maintenance concern at a neighboring property, please use the contact form or reach out to a board member directly.
