Noise & Neighborhood Courtesy

Being a considerate neighbor makes Southwood a better place for everyone

Southwood is a residential community. Maintaining a peaceful environment depends on every resident being mindful of how their activities affect those around them. The guidelines below reflect both deed restriction requirements and City of Houston noise ordinances.

Construction & Work Hours

Permitted construction, home improvement work, and landscaping that generates significant noise — including power tools, generators, compressors, and heavy equipment — must take place between 7:00 AM and 8:00 PM, Monday through Sunday, except in genuine emergency or unusual circumstances. Quiet hours outside this window are expected to be observed out of respect for neighbors.

Loud Music, Amplified Sound & Parties

Amplified sound (stereos, speakers, outdoor audio systems) should be kept at a level that does not disturb neighbors. The City of Houston’s noise ordinance prohibits sound that is plainly audible from 50 feet or more from the source after 10:00 PM on weeknights and 11:00 PM on weekends.

  • Keep outdoor gatherings and parties at a reasonable volume, especially after 9:00 PM.
  • Alert nearby neighbors in advance for planned events so expectations are set.
  • Bass-heavy music travels through walls and fences — be especially mindful of subwoofer levels at night.

Vehicle Noise

Unnecessary revving of engines, squealing tires, and using car stereos at extreme volumes on residential streets are disruptive and discourteous. Vehicles with modified exhaust systems that exceed normal noise levels should be used with restraint in residential areas. Report persistent illegal vehicle noise to Houston PD non-emergency or 311.

Dogs & Pet Noise

Persistent or excessive dog barking — especially late at night or early in the morning — can significantly disrupt the peace of the neighborhood. Pet owners are responsible for taking reasonable steps to prevent unnecessary, prolonged barking. If a neighbor’s dog is a persistent problem, try speaking with them directly before escalating to city animal control.

Trash & Recycling Pickup Days

Place bins at the curb no earlier than the evening before your scheduled pickup day, and bring empty containers back to the house the same day they are emptied. Bins left at the curb for days accumulate wind-blown litter and affect curb appeal for the entire block.

Lighting Courtesy

Exterior lighting helps deter crime and improves safety, but lights directed toward neighboring homes or that remain on through the night at high intensity can be a nuisance. Use motion-activated or shielded fixtures that focus light on your own property. Signs must not be illuminated per the deed restrictions.

Vehicle Repair & Rebuilding

Ongoing or large-scale vehicle rebuilding and mechanical repair work carried out in open view from the street is not permitted under the deed restrictions. Minor, short-term maintenance on personal vehicles is acceptable but should not become a regular activity that creates noise, oil spills, or unsightly conditions visible to neighbors.

Garage Sales

Garage sales are permitted but limited to a maximum of 2 per residence per year. Sales should be held during reasonable daytime hours and signs removed promptly after the event. Sellers are asked to keep the area tidy and collect any litter generated by shoppers.

City of Houston Noise Resources

  • Non-emergency noise complaints: Call 311 or visit houstontx.gov/311
  • Emergency or dangerous situations: Call 911
  • Animal control (barking dogs): Houston BARC — (713) 229-7300
