Parking & Right-of-Way Considerations

Rules and courtesies that keep our streets safe and accessible

Thoughtful parking habits protect neighborhood safety, maintain the visual character of our streets, and respect neighbors’ access. The deed restrictions and City of Houston ordinances together govern how and where vehicles may be parked within the Southwood Addition.

Where Vehicles May Be Parked

All vehicles must be parked in one of the following locations:

  • Inside a garage — the preferred option when space allows.
  • On an approved driveway — a concrete surface meeting City of Houston specifications (minimum 4″ thick; walks from street curb to residence must be at least 4 feet wide).
  • On the street curb — parallel to the curb and within legally marked areas.

Vehicles may not be parked on lawns, in yards, or on unpaved surfaces.

No Double Parking

Double parking — stopping or parking a vehicle so that it partially or fully blocks traffic lanes, fire hydrants, or another legally parked vehicle — is prohibited and may result in a citation from the City of Houston.

Commercial & Oversized Vehicles

18-wheelers, tractor-trailers, and other commercial heavy vehicles may not be parked on residential lots or on residential streets within the Southwood Addition except during active, permitted loading and unloading. Residents who regularly receive commercial deliveries should coordinate delivery times to minimize street impact.

Inoperable & Abandoned Vehicles

Inoperable or unlicensed vehicles must not be stored in the open on any lot. If a vehicle is not drivable, it should be stored inside a garage or removed from the property. The City of Houston’s 311 service can be called to report abandoned vehicles on public streets.

Vehicle Sales

Vehicle sales by individual owners are limited to 2 vehicles per owner per year. Vehicles offered for sale should not be staged in front yards, parkways, or on the street in a manner that creates a commercial appearance. “For Sale” signs displayed in vehicle windows must comply with the sign restrictions in the deed restrictions (maximum 6 sq ft face, no illumination).

Right-of-Way Maintenance

The parkway — the strip of land between the sidewalk and the street curb — is city right-of-way, but adjacent property owners are responsible for keeping it mowed and free of debris. Avoid placing permanent structures, large planters, or unauthorized obstacles in the parkway.

  • Keep parkway grass mowed to a height consistent with your lawn.
  • Do not park vehicles on the parkway grass, as this damages turf and irrigation.
  • Avoid planting trees or shrubs in the right-of-way without City of Houston approval.

Sidewalk Accessibility

Sidewalks must remain clear and accessible at all times. Do not block sidewalks with parked vehicles, stored items, or overgrown vegetation. Residents are responsible for maintaining the sidewalk adjacent to their property in a safe, passable condition.

City of Houston Parking Resources

For issues involving illegally parked vehicles on public streets, abandoned cars, or blocked driveways, contact the City of Houston:

  • Call 311 (City of Houston non-emergency services)
  • Visit houstontx.gov/311 to submit a service request online
