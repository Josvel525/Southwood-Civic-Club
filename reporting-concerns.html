Reporting Concerns & Next Steps

What to do when you observe a potential deed restriction or community standards issue

Keeping Southwood a clean, safe, and welcoming neighborhood is a shared responsibility. When you observe something that may violate the deed restrictions or community standards, there are constructive steps you can take. The goal is always to resolve issues amicably and preserve good neighborly relationships.

Step 1 — Review the Deed Restrictions

Before reporting a concern, familiarize yourself with the relevant sections of the deed restrictions to confirm that the activity in question is actually prohibited. Many issues that appear to be violations turn out to be permitted uses or fall within an exception. You can read the full governing documents on the Deed Restrictions page.

Step 2 — Try a Friendly Conversation

In many cases, a neighbor may be unaware that something on their property is a concern. A polite, friendly conversation is often the quickest and most effective solution. Approach the conversation with the assumption that the person wants to be a good neighbor — most people do.

  • Choose a calm time to speak, not in the heat of frustration.
  • Be specific about what you observed and which guideline it may affect.
  • Give the neighbor a reasonable opportunity to correct the issue before escalating.

Step 3 — Contact the Southwood Civic Club Board

If direct conversation is not possible or does not resolve the issue, contact the Civic Club board. Under the deed restrictions, any owner of a lot or the Association may enforce the deed restrictions to the extent permitted by Texas law.

When contacting the board, please include:

  • Your name and contact information
  • The address of the property in question
  • A clear description of the concern (what you observed, dates/times if relevant)
  • Any photos or documentation you have gathered (optional but helpful)

The board will review the concern, follow up with the property owner if appropriate, and communicate next steps to you.

Step 4 — City of Houston Code Enforcement

Some issues — such as overgrown lots, abandoned vehicles, illegal dumping, and unsafe structures — may also be enforceable by the City of Houston independently of the deed restrictions. You can report these directly to the City:

  • Houston 311: Call 311 or visit houstontx.gov/311 to submit service requests for code violations, abandoned vehicles, illegal dumping, and more.
  • Houston Public Works: For street, sidewalk, or drainage issues affecting the right-of-way.
  • Houston Police Department (non-emergency): (713) 884-3131 for criminal nuisance issues such as illegal dumping or trespassing.
  • Emergency situations: Call 911.

Understanding Enforcement Limits

The Southwood Civic Club operates as a voluntary civic organization. Enforcement of the deed restrictions may involve legal processes if a property owner does not voluntarily comply. The board will assess each situation individually. Keep in mind:

  • Failure to enforce one violation does not waive the right to enforce similar violations in the future (per the deed restrictions, Section 7.3).
  • Non-conforming uses that existed before the deed restrictions were adopted in 2007 may be grandfathered, subject to the limitations described in Article VII.
  • The board aims to resolve concerns informally whenever possible and reserves legal action as a last resort.
