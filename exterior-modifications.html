Exterior Modifications

Fencing, paint, additions, and other changes to your property’s exterior

Any exterior modification to your home — including new construction, additions, fencing, re-roofing, siding replacement, or painting — may require prior approval from the Housing & Community Development Committee. Reviewing these guidelines before starting a project can save time and help avoid disputes.

Approval Process

Before beginning most exterior construction or modification projects, submit detailed plans to the Housing & Community Development Committee. Plans should include:

  • Foundation or structural information
  • Floor plan and elevation drawings
  • Exterior materials list and color selections
  • Plot layout showing setbacks and distances from property lines

The Committee has 30 days to respond. If no response is received within 30 days, the plans are deemed approved. The review period may be extended by an additional 15 days with written notice to the applicant.

Fencing

Fences help define property boundaries and provide privacy, but they must comply with the following restrictions:

  • Barbed wire, razor wire, electrified fencing, or any other dangerous fencing material is strictly prohibited.
  • Maximum fence height near streets is 6 feet. Consult the deed restrictions for setback-specific height limits.
  • Fences must be maintained in good repair — replace broken boards, straighten leaning sections, and refinish or repaint as needed.
  • All fence materials and colors should be consistent with the character of the neighborhood.

Exterior Paint & Siding

When replacing or repairing siding or repainting, the new material and color must be consistent with the existing exterior in type, quality, size, and appearance. Dramatic color changes or mismatched materials may require Committee approval.

  • Replacement siding must match the existing material (brick, stucco, hardiplank, etc.).
  • At least 75% of all exterior wall surfaces (excluding doors and windows) must be masonry or brick veneer for new construction or full exterior replacements.
  • Repaint projects do not require formal approval but should use colors compatible with neighborhood aesthetics.

Roofing

  • Repairs: Matching the existing roof material and color does not require Committee approval.
  • Full replacement: Requires Committee approval for acceptable roofing type and quality. Replacement roofing must carry a minimum 25-year warranty.
  • Roof pitch must be between 4:12 (minimum) and 9:12 (maximum).

Additions & New Construction

Room additions, garage conversions, new accessory structures, and similar projects must meet the following standards:

  • Setbacks: Front — 25 ft (or consistent with majority of block); Side — 3 ft minimum; Rear — 5 ft minimum.
  • Building height: Maximum 35 feet without special approval. First-floor wall plate maximum 10 feet.
  • Minimum square footage for new single-family homes:
    • One-story: 2,000 sq ft
    • One-and-one-half story: 2,250 sq ft (upper floor must be at least 30% of ground floor)
    • Two-story: 2,500 sq ft (upper floor must be at least 70% of ground floor)
  • Foundation must be concrete slab or pier-and-beam construction.
  • Construction hours are limited to 7:00 AM – 8:00 PM, except in emergency or unusual circumstances.

Antennae & Satellite Dishes

Standard television antennae and satellite dishes are permitted but must be positioned toward the rear of the property to minimize visibility from the street whenever technically feasible.

Variance Requests

If your proposed project cannot meet one or more of the restrictions above, you may submit a variance request to the Housing & Community Development Committee. The Committee has 30 days to grant or deny the variance. Silence for 30 days constitutes approval.

Need Help?

Contact the board with any questions before starting your project. Early communication prevents delays and helps ensure a smooth approval process.
